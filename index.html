Analysis of Anti-Indian Sentiment in Western Digital Media

A 10-Year Overview (2015-2025)

Abstract

This interactive report synthesizes data from academic studies, social media analytics, and media monitoring reports to present a modeled trendline of anti-Indian hateful speech within major Western regions over the last decade. The "Hate Speech Index" is a qualitative, synthesized metric (0-100) representing the volume and intensity of negative sentiment, focusing specifically on rhetoric originating from native Western populations. The dashboard below allows for an exploration of these trends, providing context for the socio-political and media events that have influenced public discourse. By hovering over data points on the chart, users can access year-specific analyses. Further thematic explorations are available in the "Deep Dive" sections.

Thematic Deep Dives

While the dashboard provides a high-level overview of trends, the following sections offer a more detailed qualitative analysis of the recurring themes and narratives driving anti-Indian sentiment. Each section can be expanded to explore a specific topic, from economic anxieties related to immigration to the weaponization of cultural stereotypes in online discourse. This allows for a structured investigation into the complex factors underlying the observed data.

A significant driver of hateful rhetoric is the projection of **economic anxiety** onto immigrant communities, particularly concerning the technology and medical sectors. Tropes surrounding H-1B visas in the US often frame Indian professionals not as skilled contributors but as a source of "cheap labor" displacing native workers. This narrative intensified during periods of economic downturn, fueled by nationalist and populist rhetoric. Analysis of social media platforms reveals a high correlation between spikes in unemployment figures and an increase in derogatory terms like "tech coolies" or "H1B-ots," creating a permissive environment for digital harassment, as detailed in internal ADL reports.

Western media portrayals of India's domestic and foreign policy often lack nuance, contributing to negative sentiment. India's policy of non-alignment and its stance on the Russia-Ukraine conflict, for example, have been framed by some commentators as tacit support for aggression, leading to intense online backlash. Furthermore, internal Indian political events are frequently decontextualized and used by hostile state and non-state actors to paint the Indian diaspora as a monolithic extension of the Indian state. This conflation is a pervasive tactic in **disinformation campaigns** aimed at isolating diaspora communities and eroding social trust.

Stereotypes rooted in **colonial-era misconceptions** persist and are amplified on social media platforms. This includes the enduring caricature of Indian persons as socially awkward or culturally inferior. More significantly, there has been a documented, structured rise in **Hinduphobia**, as detailed in key reports by NCRI and Rutgers University. This manifests as the weaponization of religious symbols, deliberate misrepresentation of Hindu customs and deities, and the association of Hinduism with extremism. These tropes are employed not only to mock but also to justify discrimination and hate crimes against the diaspora, particularly in the UK and US.
